The Basics of Recommendation Algorithms

Recommendation algorithms are systems that are designed to suggest items that best fit their consumers. These algorithms can be found from social media to streaming apps and suggest what ads are relevant based on the data they collect about you. An example of this would be Google's use of search data to find products that may interest you through their Google Ads product. Recommendation algorithms can be used to provide a personalized user experience as they can constantly adapt a user's suggestions based on thier activities.

The Feedback Loop

The Feedback Loop is a way of collecting information based on the number of times a user interacts with content. It is a self-reinforcing algorithm that learns the type of content you engage with and progressively gets more accurate with the more data it has on the user. Over time, users are exposed to content or products that align with their interests which creates a custom curated experience on the platform for the user.

Conspiracy Theories & Algorithmic Amplification

The algorithms utilized on many platforms are geared towards creating high engagement to increase time spent on their app. As a result, consipiracy theories often end up getting pushed to the top of recommendation lists due to them being engaging by nature. Even though it may lead people away from the truth, people may accept it as fact in terms of their beliefs algorithms prioritize boosting revenue from ads by increasing user engagement with their platform above all else which can cause misinformation to spread throughout society inadvertently.

Platform Incentives

Since platforms control what ads get seen, they may prioritize content that pockets the most money possible for them, even if it lacks accuracy. This may create an environment of false information for users which can spread under the assumption of the truth. Platforms can also create multiple tiers of ads that have benefits such as unskippable for a duration or unskippable at all. This can be dangerous because not all content is truthful and companies may be only interested in profits.

Potential for Exploitation

It is relatively easy to manipulate information and promote it through ad services. They often advertise false facts or deceive you into purchasing products that do not do their intended function. Bad actors can take advantage of this and potentially profit of it just by artificially boosting the promotion by purchasing ads or using bots to boost engagement in order for it to reach to real audiences. In conclusion, it is important to be aware of how recommendation algorithms work as well as how they can be manipulated for illicit purposes.
